Hello, my name is Nicolas and I sometimes write recreative programs. You can electronic mail me via nico at puffer dot fish. Instead of writing long and useless articles, I have written here concise and thoughtfull sentences I believe are true about computer programming and system engineering. Copyright twenty fifteen.

  1. Hardware must always be the reality to target.
  2. Programming a computer will never be a natural activity.
  3. Notation is too often overlooked, have a look at APL.
  4. If there's a mismatch between the implementation and the specification then the implementation is right.
  5. Function composition, not application, is the modularity to reach for.
  6. Bad abstractions lie, good abstractions hide.
  7. Interacting is the machine obeying the human while programming is the human obeying the machine.
  8. A function is nothing but a sequence of instructions manipulating numbers.
  9. The compiler is amazing but never to be fully trusted.
  10. Most programming languages and operating systems are lying.
