{us,modd,design}ers

May 2025 — We can classify the people interacting with a system in three groups. No group is better than another one and we constantly change group.

  1. Users act upon the system to achieve their goal, reliably, without too much thinking. They know only what they do not like, which includes stupid defaults, invalid documentation, and tasks that could be automated. Give them play dough, not diamand.
  2. Modders make the system do actions its creators haven't thought of, using only the system's primitives. It is an artistic expression which is helped by easy to use and malleable primitives and without aggressive licensing.
  3. Designers create a system from the system, modifying its outsides, providing new primitives. They design a system, and they do it best with access to the blueprints.
